Samuel Oschin Planetarium

The Samuel Oschin Planetarium astronomical projection hall features state-of-the-art technology that provides world-class science education programs to audiences of all ages. The aluminum dome with a 22m high hole is one of the largest astronomical projection halls in the world. As part of the maintenance work of the Observatory, the 1964 Zeiss Mark IV, the old star projector in the astronomical projection room, was replaced by the cutting-edge Zeiss Mark IX Universarium. The venue's infamous wooden headrest has been replaced with custom seats that lean back.
